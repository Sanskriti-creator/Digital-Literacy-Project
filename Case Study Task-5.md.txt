Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money or sharing sensitive banking details. It usually happens through phone calls, fake links, or social media messages.

In a typical scenario, the fraudster contacts the victim pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or even a buyer on an online marketplace. First, they gain the victim’s trust by sounding professional or offering an attractive deal. Next, they ask the victim to scan a QR code or click on a payment link, claiming it is required to “receive” money. However, in reality, scanning the QR code or approving the request results in money being debited from the victim’s account. Sometimes, they also ask for OTPs or UPI PINs, which gives them direct access to the account.

Students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online systems. In many cases, victims find it difficult to recover their money.

This case highlights the importance of digital awareness. Users should never share OTPs or PINs, and should remember that receiving money does not require scanning a QR code. Being cautious can help prevent such frauds.
